Robert James Williams

Set Design & Choreography

✦

I never pictured my furniture design career blending into family life like this. After thirty years crafting pieces for Biltmore, Hearst Castle, Versailles Foundation, and Habersham, I got pulled into the theater world—first building sets, then handling choreography and staging. It started simple: my kids auditioned, I volunteered. Soon I was blocking scenes for Oklahoma, rigging props for The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe, even pacing the stage for Pride and Prejudice. Turns out, designing a throne on paper feels just like setting one up for a high-school king. The payoff? Seeing them shine on a stage I helped shape. That mix of craft and connection—it's the best thing I've built.
